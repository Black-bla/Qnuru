# **FOUNDER AGREEMENT & COFOUNDER CONTRACT**

This Founder Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into on **[Date]**, by and between the undersigned founders (“Founders”) for the purpose of governing the formation, operation, and growth of **[Company Name] Ltd**, a company incorporated under the laws of the **Republic of Kenya** (“Company”).

---

## **1. PURPOSE OF THE COMPANY**

The Company is established to operate as an integrated digital services and talent platform, including but not limited to:

- Software development, POS systems, CRM, automation, and AI-enabled tools
    
- Website design, UI/UX, and digital infrastructure
    
- Digital marketing, social media management, branding, and media production
    
- A vetted subcontractor and talent delivery network operating under the Company brand
    
- A future skills academy offering short, market-driven digital and technology courses
    

The Company shall operate independently, even where one or more Founders previously operated under separate brands.

---

## **2. FOUNDERS & ROLES**

The Company shall initially have **three (3) Cofounders**, all equal in founding status.

### **Initial Roles (Flexible & Non-Exclusive)**

- **Founder A** –Systems architect
    
- **Founder B** – Growth and sales
    
- **Founder C** –Brand and media
    

Roles may **overlap, evolve, or be reassigned** by mutual agreement based on company needs. No role confers unilateral ownership or superiority.

Each Founder commits to acting in the **best interests of the Company at all times**.

---

## **3. EQUITY STRUCTURE & VESTING**

### **3.1 Initial Equity**

Unless otherwise agreed in writing:

- Equity shall be split **equally (33.33% each)** among the Founders.
    

### **3.2 Vesting**

- Founder equity shall vest over **4 years**, with a **12-month cliff**.
    
- If a Founder exits before the cliff, **all unvested shares revert to the Company**.
    
- After the cliff, shares vest monthly.
    

### **3.3 Dilution**

- All Founders agree to reasonable dilution in the event of:
    
    - Fundraising
        
    - Strategic partnerships
        
    - Employee or contractor equity incentives
        
- Dilution shall apply **proportionally** unless otherwise agreed.
    

---

## **4. DECISION-MAKING & GOVERNANCE**

### **4.1 Day-to-Day Operations**

- Each Founder may independently execute tasks within their functional role.
    
- No Founder may bind the Company to significant financial or legal obligations without approval.
    

### **4.2 Reserved Matters (Require Majority or Unanimous Approval)**

Unanimous approval required for:

- Equity changes
    
- Sale of IP or core assets
    
- Company dissolution
    

Majority approval required for:

- Pricing strategy changes
    
- Hiring senior staff or long-term contractors
    
- Entering new business lines
    

### **4.3 Tie-Breaking**

- In the event of a deadlock, the matter shall proceed to mediation.
    

---

## **5. TIME COMMITMENT & CONDUCT**

- Each Founder commits to **reasonable and consistent contribution**.
    
- Gross negligence, fraud, reputational harm, or persistent non-performance constitutes breach.
    
- Founders must act professionally with clients, subcontractors, and partners.
    

---

## **6. COMPENSATION & PROFIT DISTRIBUTION**

### **6.1 Salaries**

- Founder salaries are **optional**, subject to Company cash flow.
    
- Priority is sustainability and reinvestment.
    

### **6.2 Profit Sharing**

- Profits may be distributed quarterly or annually upon agreement.
    
- The Company may retain profits for growth at majority approval.
    

### **6.3 Expenses**

- Reasonable business expenses are reimbursable with documentation.
    

---

## **7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY (IP)**

### **7.1 Company Ownership**

All work produced by any Founder relating to:

- Code, software, automation
    
- Designs, media, branding
    
- Training materials, systems, processes
    

shall be deemed **“Work Made for Hire”** and is the **exclusive property of the Company**.

### **7.2 Pre-Existing IP**

- Any pre-existing IP contributed must be disclosed.
    
- Such IP is either assigned or licensed to the Company as agreed.
    

---

## **8. CLIENTS, SUBCONTRACTORS & NON-CIRCUMVENTION**

- All clients are **Company clients**, not personal clients.
    
- Founders shall not:
    
    - Direct clients to personal businesses
        
    - Bypass the Company to work directly with subcontractors or clients
        
- All subcontractors operate **under Company branding and authority**.
    

This clause survives termination.

---

## **9. CONFIDENTIALITY**

Founders shall keep confidential all:

- Client data
    
- Pricing, strategies, systems
    
- Contractor databases
    
- Internal operations
    

This obligation continues **indefinitely**, including after exit.

---

## **10. NON-COMPETE & NON-SOLICITATION**

For **12 months after exit**, within **Kenya**:

- Founders shall not directly compete with the Company
    
- Founders shall not solicit Company clients, subcontractors, or students
    

These restrictions are intended to be **reasonable and enforceable**.

---

## **11. EXIT, REMOVAL & BUY-BACK**

### **11.1 Voluntary Exit**

- Unvested shares revert to the Company.
    
- Vested shares subject to right of first refusal by remaining Founders.
    

### **11.2 Forced Removal**

Grounds include:

- Fraud or misconduct
    
- Breach of confidentiality
    
- Reputational harm
    
- Persistent non-performance
    

### **11.3 Valuation**

- Fair market value determined by:
    
    - Independent valuation, or
        
    - Agreed revenue multiple, or
        
    - Net asset value (fallback)
        

---

## **12. DISPUTE RESOLUTION**

Disputes shall follow this order:

1. Good faith negotiation
    
2. Mediation
    
3. Arbitration in Kenya
    
4. Courts of Kenya (as last resort)
    

---

## **13. FAILURE & DISSOLUTION**

If the Company winds down:

- Assets and IP belong to the Company
    
- Liabilities settled first
    
- Remaining assets distributed by equity share
    

---

## **14. GOVERNING LAW**

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed under the laws of the **Republic of Kenya**.

---

## **15. ENTIRE AGREEMENT**

This document constitutes the entire agreement between the Founders and supersedes prior discussions.

## **Dissolution Clause**

### **1. Triggers for Dissolution**

The Company may be dissolved under any of the following circumstances:  
1.1. Voluntary decision by unanimous consent of all founders.  
1.2. Failure to meet critical performance indicators (KPIs) or agreed milestones within a defined period.  
1.3. Bankruptcy, insolvency, or legal/regulatory action making continued operations impossible.  
1.4. Mutual agreement following a deadlock in decision-making or irreconcilable disputes.

---

### **2. Equity and Ownership**

2.1. Upon dissolution, all company equity shall be valued at fair market value, including tangible and intangible assets.  
2.2. Founders will receive their proportional share of company equity based on initial agreements.  
2.3. Any partially developed projects, intellectual property, or software shall be allocated based on contribution or pre-agreed ownership percentages, unless otherwise specified.  
2.4. Outstanding obligations to investors or third parties will take priority before distributing equity or assets.

---

### **3. Debt and Liabilities**

3.1. The company shall settle all debts, contractual obligations, and liabilities to clients, contractors, employees, and vendors before equity or asset distribution.  
3.2. Founders are collectively responsible for ensuring the proper winding up of financial obligations, unless liability is legally limited to company assets.

---

### **4. Roles During Dissolution**

4.1. Each founder shall continue to perform duties necessary to wind down operations professionally, including:

- Communicating with clients and contractors about the dissolution.
    
- Ensuring proper handover or closure of projects.
    
- Securing and archiving company records, systems, and IP.  
    4.2. A designated founder may be appointed to coordinate the dissolution process.
    

---

### **5. Dispute Resolution**

5.1. Any disagreement regarding the dissolution process, valuation, or asset distribution shall first be submitted to mediation.  
5.2. If mediation fails, disputes shall be resolved through binding arbitration under the laws of [Your Jurisdiction].

---

### **6. Non-Compete & Non-Solicitation**

6.1. For a period of [12–24 months] following dissolution, founders shall not:

- Solicit former clients of the company directly for competing services.
    
- Recruit or employ contractors or employees from the company without written consent.  
    6.2. Exceptions may be negotiated in writing.
    

---

### **7. IP and Technology Rights**

7.1. All company-developed intellectual property, software, tools, and systems shall be treated as follows:

- Owned IP shall be distributed according to ownership percentages or agreements.
    
- Licensed IP must revert to the original licensor or as specified in licensing terms.  
    7.2. Founders may agree to jointly license or sell IP to third parties if mutually beneficial.
    

---

### **8. Timeline and Process**

8.1. **Announcement:** Founders formally notify stakeholders of intent to dissolve.  
8.2. **Evaluation:** Complete financial audit, asset inventory, and project status assessment.  
8.3. **Settlements:** Pay debts, close contracts, and secure IP.  
8.4. **Distribution:** Allocate remaining assets, equity, and IP according to agreed percentages.  
8.5. **Closure:** Cancel registrations, licenses, and company accounts; archive all records.

---

**Key Principle:**

> This clause ensures a fair, orderly, and legally enforceable process for dissolving the company while protecting founders’ interests, IP, and client relationships.




## **Personal Relationships and Conflict Clause**

### **1. Separation of Personal and Professional Life**

1.1. All founders, executives, and contributors shall maintain a clear boundary between personal relationships and company operations.  
1.2. Decisions related to company strategy, projects, finance, clients, contractors, or IP must remain **objective and role-based**, and shall not be influenced by personal relationships.

---

### **2. Disclosure Requirement**

2.1. Any romantic or intimate relationship involving a founder, executive, or key contributor that could potentially create a conflict of interest must be disclosed to the board or co-founders in writing.  
2.2. Disclosure is intended to allow **proactive management** and prevent personal dynamics from impacting company operations.

---

### **3. Conflict Management**

3.1. If a personal relationship begins to affect business decisions, work performance, or company culture, the affected parties must:

- Notify the co-founders immediately
    
- Agree on a **mitigation plan** (e.g., reassignment of responsibilities, temporary separation from sensitive projects, or external mediation)  
    3.2. If the conflict cannot be resolved internally, it shall be escalated to an agreed-upon **independent mediator** or arbitrator.
    

---

### **4. No Retaliation**

4.1. Termination of a personal relationship shall **not give either party the right to disrupt, damage, or otherwise interfere** with the company, its IP, or ongoing projects.  
4.2. Any attempt to do so may be considered a breach of this agreement, with remedies including legal action and financial restitution.

---

### **5. Contractor and Contributor Limits**

5.1. Romantic partners or personal associates may participate in company projects **only as contractors or short-term contributors**, with clearly defined scope, deliverables, and IP ownership.  
5.2. No personal relationships shall automatically confer equity, decision-making power, or access to confidential company information.

---

### **6. Enforcement**

6.1. Founders retain the right to **reassign responsibilities, restrict access to systems, or terminate contracts** if personal conflicts threaten company operations.  
6.2. All parties acknowledge that **company interest supersedes personal considerations** at all times.

---

**Key Principle:**

> Protect the company by separating personal relationships from operations, requiring transparency, and defining enforceable mechanisms for conflict resolution and accountability.

## 3. MEDIATION: FIRST EXTERNAL LAYER (MANDATORY)

### **Appointed Independent Mediator**

Your agreement should explicitly name or define the mediator.

**Best Kenyan options:**

- **Chartered Institute of Arbitrators (CIArb Kenya)**
    
- A named **law firm or advocate**
    
- A professional business mediator agreed in advance
    

Example clause (recommended):

> “Any dispute not resolved internally within fourteen (14) days shall be referred to mediation under the rules of the Chartered Institute of Arbitrators (Kenya Branch).”

This prevents emotional escalation.

- Decision is binding
    
- Faster and cheaper than court
    

This ensures disputes **end**, not drag.
