# **Company Founders Meeting Agenda**

**Date:** Today Afternoon  
**Duration:** 1.5–2 hours  
**Location:** Thairo’s house / Zoom (if needed)

---

## **1. Opening & Purpose (5–10 min)**

- Welcome and brief introductions
    
- Restate the **company vision**: full-service digital solutions, academy, and long-term growth
    
- Emphasize **meeting objectives**: decide on company name, finalize founder roles, confirm key deliverables, and align on execution-first approach
    

---

## **2. Company Name Discussion (15–20 min)**

- Present potential names (Kenyan, brandable, reflects combined vision)
    
- Discuss pros and cons of each name
    
- Shortlist top 3–5 options
    
- Decide on **action plan for final selection** (vote, defer to another session, etc.)
    

---

## **3. Founder Roles & Responsibilities (20–25 min)**

- Present proposed roles and hierarchy:
    
    - CEO  – Brand, client acquisition, academy promotion
        
    - COO / Strategic Officer  – Pricing, partnerships, client relations, operational guidance
        
    - CTO   – Systems, infrastructure, automation, technical oversight
        
- Clarify **authority, reporting lines, and decision-making power**
    
- Confirm **consensus and comfort level** for all founders
    

---

## **4. Key Deliverables & Initial Focus (25–30 min)**

- Present **founder-specific deliverables** for first 3–6 months
    
- Discuss **client acquisition strategy** and pilot packages (Launch, Scale, Dominate)
    
- Confirm **academy development plan** and potential early courses
- Confirm work space/gigz
    
- Decide **short-term KPIs**: number of clients, revenue targets, project delivery metrics
    

---

## **5. Subcontractor & Technical Contributor Strategy (15–20 min)**

- Outline plan for hiring or testing subcontractors
    
- Discuss optional technical contributor / developer reporting directly to CTO
    
- Clarify **scope, incentives, and IP ownership**
    

---

## **6. Market & Risk Considerations (10–15 min)**

- Present competitor landscape and preliminary research plan
    
- Highlight AI disruption risk and your **results-focused defense**
    
- Discuss strategy for staying agile, execution-first, and maintaining quality
    

---

## **7. Next Steps & Action Items (10–15 min)**

- Assign immediate tasks and responsibilities:
    
    - Finalize company name
        
    - Market research assignments
        
    - MVP client outreach and pilot projects
        
    - Documentation of SOPs and deliverables
        
- Schedule follow-up meeting to review **progress and early pilot results**
    

---

## **8. Closing (5 min)**

- Summarize decisions made
    
- Reaffirm shared vision and execution-first mindset
    
- Confirm next steps and responsible parties



# Other

---

## **1. Legal & Governance Structure**

- **Company registration & ownership split**: clearly define equity percentages, voting rights, and decision-making authority
    
- **Contracts for founders, contractors, and academy instructors**: IP ownership, confidentiality, deliverables
    
- **Conflict resolution mechanisms**: who mediates disputes, how decisions are escalated
    

---

## **2. Financial Planning & Sustainability**

- **Revenue projections**: per package, per client, per month
    
- **Cash flow management**: especially if you’re subcontracting and paying external developers
    
- **Contingency planning**: what happens if client payments are late or projects fail
    

---

## **3. Client Delivery & Quality Assurance**

- Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for each package
    
- QA processes to ensure reliability, especially with subcontractors
    
- Service level agreements (SLAs) to protect both clients and your brand
    

---

## **4. Brand Positioning & Storytelling**

- How the company differentiates from Fiverr, Workke, and global AI tools
    
- How you communicate **trust, results, and professionalism**
    
- Marketing narrative for both the academy and service packages
    

---

## **5. Growth & Scaling Strategy**

- When to hire full-time employees vs. contractors
    
- How to expand client base beyond early adopters
    
- Metrics for scaling packages, academy, and subcontractor network
    

---

## **6. Risk Management**

- AI disruption (already discussed)
    
- Over-reliance on one or two clients
    
- Contractor non-performance or poaching by clients
    
- Regulatory changes for payments, data, or digital services
    

---

## **7. Knowledge Management**

- Documenting processes so subcontractors and future hires can work effectively
    
- Maintaining a knowledge base for tech, marketing, and client delivery
    

---

## **8. Company Culture & Values**

- Setting expectations for work ethic, communication, and collaboration
    
- Preventing over-identification with “vision perfection” vs. customer delivery
    
- Ensuring all founders are aligned on **risk-taking, ownership, and accountability**
