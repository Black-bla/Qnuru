Here’s a **practical and professional approach** to drafting a contract for a technical contributor like him, covering scope, IP, and incentives while keeping it low-risk:

---

## **1. Contract Essentials**

### **A. Parties & Purpose**

- Clearly state **he is a contractor reporting directly to the CTO**
    
- Specify the company name and his role as **Technical Contributor / Contractor**
    
- Describe the **purpose**: building, maintaining, and supporting infrastructure, POS, automation systems, or other technical projects assigned by CTO
    

---

### **B. Scope of Work**

- Define tasks clearly:
    
    - System development, integration, troubleshooting
        
    - Backend support for websites, POS, automation
        
    - Optional contribution to technical innovations under CTO guidance
        
- Specify **project or milestone basis** to avoid ambiguity
    
- Include a clause: “Tasks outside scope require written agreement from CTO”
    

---

### **C. Reporting & Communication**

- Reports **only to CTO**
    
- CTO sets priorities, deadlines, and milestones
    
- No direct reporting to CEO or COO
    
- Attendance in company-wide meetings only when requested
    

---

### **D. Compensation & Incentives**

- Fixed payment per project/milestone OR hourly rate
    
- Optional **bonus or revenue share** tied to delivered projects
    
- Optional **future equity or stake** if milestones are met
    
- Payment terms clearly stated (e.g., upon delivery, end of month, or milestone completion)
    

---

### **E. Intellectual Property (IP)**

- All work produced for the company is **company-owned**
    
- Include a clause:
    
    > “All source code, designs, systems, scripts, and related materials produced during engagement are the sole property of the company. Contractor waives any claim to ownership or reuse of work produced for the company.”
    
- Specify that he **cannot use company IP in personal projects** without written approval
    
- Include confidentiality clause (non-disclosure of proprietary systems, client data, internal processes)
    

---

### **F. Confidentiality**

- Must keep all technical details, systems, client data, and internal processes confidential
    
- Violation can result in termination and legal action
    

---

### **G. Termination**

- Either party can terminate with written notice (e.g., 2–4 weeks)
    
- Include termination clauses for:
    
    - Breach of confidentiality
        
    - Missed deadlines / poor performance
        
    - Unauthorized sharing or use of IP
        

---

### **H. Dispute Resolution**

- Mediation through CTO (first)
    
- Escalation to company founders if unresolved
    
- Kenyan law as governing jurisdiction
    

---

### **I. Miscellaneous**

- Independent contractor status (no employee benefits unless agreed)
    
- Intellectual property rights clearly assigned to the company
    
- Work-for-hire clause
    
- Optional clause on future equity milestones
    

---

## **2. Why This Works**

- Keeps **governance clean**: he answers only to you
    
- Protects **company IP and proprietary systems**
    
- Offers **incentives** without giving cofounder equity upfront
    
- Defines **boundaries clearly**, preventing conflicts with CEO or COO
    
- Flexible for scaling or conversion to full-time/cofounder if needed later
