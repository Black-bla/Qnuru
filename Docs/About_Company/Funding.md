## **1. Funding Strategy: Bootstrapping with Revenue**

- **Primary funding source:** Paying clients from MVP packages (Launch, Scale, Dominate)
    
- **Secondary source:** Early academy signups, workshops, or consulting gigs
    
- **Cost control:** Keep overhead low; founders initially execute most work themselves
    

**Advantages:**

- You retain full equity and control
    
- Forces a focus on **client acquisition and deliverable results**
    
- Limits dependency on external investors
    

**Risks:**

- Cash flow is unpredictable—slow client acquisition can stall growth
    
- Scaling (subcontractors, marketing, academy) may be slower
    
- Risk of overextending before revenue stabilizes
    

---

## **2. Financial Planning**

- **Break-even analysis:**
    
    - Identify minimum clients needed per month to cover basic operations
        
    - Example: If you need Ksh 100,000 to run the company and deliver 5 projects → each project must cover at least Ksh 20,000 (Launch package)
        
- **Revenue allocation:**
    
    - **Operations & contractor payouts**: 50–60% of revenue
        
    - **Marketing & growth**: 20–25%
        
    - **Reinvestment / savings**: 15–20% for future tools, academy, or expansion
        
- **Cash buffer:** Maintain a small reserve (even Ksh 30–50k) to handle delays in client payments
    

---

## **3. Funding Timeline & Milestones**

|Milestone|Funding Goal|Notes|
|---|---|---|
|MVP Launch|Cover founders’ operating costs|Target 5 paying clients within first month|
|Post-Pilot|Expand marketing & academy|Use 20–30% of revenue from first clients to promote growth|
|Subcontractor Rollout|Hire 1–2 external contractors|Only once revenue is stable and repeatable|
|Scaling Academy|Invest in course creation & paid marketing|Reinvest profits from client services|

---

## **4. Optional Supplementary Sources**

- **Small grants / competitions:** Local entrepreneurship programs, government innovation funds
    
- **Microloans:** For small equipment or software licenses
    
- **Strategic partners:** Agencies, corporates, or NGOs willing to fund pilot projects in exchange for deliverables
    

---

### **Key Principle**

> **Revenue validates the model before external capital.**  
> Focus first on delivering measurable results, building trust, and generating predictable cash flow. Only then expand services, hire subcontractors, or scale the academy.
