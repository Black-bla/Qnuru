# **Neuron Tide Anti-Cutoff System**

---

## **1. Legal Safeguards**

### **A. Client Contracts**

Include clear clauses:

**Non-Circumvention:**

> “The client agrees not to directly hire or engage any Neuron Tide subcontractor introduced during this engagement for a period of 12 months after project completion, without prior written consent from Neuron Tide. Any breach incurs a buyout fee of [X% of project value].”

**IP & Deliverables Ownership:**

> “All project deliverables remain the property of Neuron Tide until full payment is received. Subcontractors’ work is submitted under Neuron Tide’s brand and intellectual property rights.”

**Retainer Clause (if applicable):**

> Clients retain Neuron Tide for ongoing support and updates. Early termination requires written notice and settlement of outstanding fees.

---

### **B. Subcontractor Agreements**

- NDA: Confidentiality of client info
    
- Non-Solicitation: Subcontractors cannot approach Neuron Tide clients independently
    
- IP Assignment: All work created belongs to Neuron Tide
    
- Violation Penalty: Subcontractor agrees to reimburse Neuron Tide for lost revenue if breach occurs
    

**Optional:** Include “Performance & Loyalty Bonuses” for subcontractors to incentivize compliance and long-term collaboration.

---

## **2. Operational Safeguards**

### **A. Centralized Communication**

- All client conversations routed through **company-managed channels** (email, Slack, Trello/ClickUp)
    
- Subcontractors receive **task-specific instructions**, not direct client access
    

### **B. Controlled Workflows**

- Use **company-owned platforms** for deliverables:
    
    - WordPress, POS dashboards, CMS, cloud drives
        
- Deliver packaged outputs (e.g., compiled website, video render, CSV exports)
    
- Subcontractor cannot directly hand over working files to clients
    

### **C. Retainer & Milestone Structure**

- Break payment into **initial setup + milestones + monthly retainer**
    
- Only release files after milestone approval
    
- Continuous retainer ensures ongoing dependency on Neuron Tide
    

---

## **3. Value Proposition Safeguards**

- Position Neuron Tide as **essential**:
    
    - Brand trust and “Neurontide stamp” on every deliverable
        
    - Quality vetting of all subcontractors
        
    - Project management and integration services
        
    - Support & maintenance
        

Clients pay for **coordination and trust**, not just raw labor — bypassing becomes unattractive.

---

## **4. Revenue & Retention Strategy**

1. **Client Retainers**
    
    - Continuous updates, analytics, automation tweaks, content delivery
        
    - Makes it costly and risky for the client to cut out Neuron Tide
        
2. **Subcontractor Loyalty Incentives**
    
    - Premium rate for vetted, long-term contributors
        
    - Certification & Neuron Tide branding increases their earning potential
        
    - Referral bonuses for bringing in other qualified talent
        
3. **Integrated Automation & SaaS**
    
    - Host systems under Neuron Tide accounts
        
    - Subcontractors cannot “detach” systems without company oversight
        

---

## **5. Enforcement & Risk Management**

- Maintain **documented proof** of all communications and deliverables
    
- Keep **audit trail** of subcontractor work
    
- Regular check-ins with subcontractors to reinforce company ownership
    
- Clearly communicate consequences of breach to both clients and subcontractors
    

---

### **6. Summary Flow**

1. Client signs contract → NDA & non-circumvention clause
    
2. Subcontractor signs agreement → IP + NDA + non-solicitation
    
3. Tasks assigned through **internal workflow**
    
4. Deliverables packaged and branded → client receives only Neuron Tide output
    
5. Retainer/ongoing support maintains dependency
    
6. Internal review ensures quality and reduces incentive to bypass
    

---

**Bottom line:**

> By combining **legal agreements, centralized workflows, retainer structures, and brand value**, Neuron Tide becomes the **trusted middleman that clients and subcontractors rely on**. Cutting out the company becomes **difficult, risky, and unattractive**.
